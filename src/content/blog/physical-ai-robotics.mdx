# Physical AI / Robotics

Physical AI represents the frontier where artificial intelligence meets the real world, enabling robots and embodied systems to perceive, reason, and act in physical environments.

## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI encompasses AI systems that interact with the physical world through sensors and actuators, requiring understanding of physics, spatial reasoning, and real-time decision-making.

## Key Areas

### Robot Manipulation

Teaching robots to grasp, move, and manipulate objects with dexterity and precision.

### Navigation and Locomotion

Enabling robots to move through complex environments, from wheeled platforms to humanoid walking.

### Perception

Processing sensor data (cameras, lidar, tactile sensors) to understand the physical environment.

## Learning Approaches

### Imitation Learning

Robots learn by observing and mimicking human demonstrations.

### Reinforcement Learning

Learning optimal behaviors through trial and error in simulated or real environments.

### Sim-to-Real Transfer

Training in simulation and transferring learned skills to physical robots.

## Applications

- Manufacturing and logistics
- Healthcare and assistive robotics
- Autonomous vehicles
- Household robots
- Search and rescue

## Challenges

- Sample efficiency in learning
- Sim-to-real gap
- Safety in human environments
- Generalization across tasks and environments

## Future Directions

The integration of foundation models with robotics promises more capable and adaptable physical AI systems.
