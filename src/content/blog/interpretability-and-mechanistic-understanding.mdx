# Interpretability and Mechanistic Understanding

Interpretability research aims to understand how AI systems work internally, moving beyond treating them as black boxes to reveal the mechanisms underlying their behavior.

## What is Mechanistic Interpretability?

Mechanistic interpretability seeks to reverse-engineer neural networks, understanding the specific computations and representations they use to process information and produce outputs.

## Why It Matters

### Safety

Understanding how models work helps identify potential failure modes and risks.

### Trust

Mechanistic understanding builds confidence in AI system behavior.

### Improvement

Insights into model internals guide architectural and training improvements.

### Science

Understanding artificial neural networks may yield insights into intelligence itself.

## Key Concepts

### Features and Representations

Identifying what concepts or features neurons and layers encode.

### Circuits

Understanding how groups of neurons work together to implement specific computations.

### Superposition

How networks encode more features than they have dimensions through distributed representations.

### Polysemanticity

Individual neurons responding to multiple, seemingly unrelated concepts.

## Research Approaches

### Probing

Training simple classifiers on internal representations to identify encoded information.

### Activation Analysis

Studying what inputs cause specific activation patterns.

### Causal Interventions

Modifying internal activations to understand their causal role.

### Visualization

Creating visual representations of learned features and attention patterns.

## Applications

- Identifying and removing harmful behaviors
- Understanding model capabilities and limitations
- Debugging model failures
- Guiding model development

## Challenges

- Scale of modern models
- Superposition and polysemanticity
- Validating interpretability claims
- Translating findings into actionable insights
