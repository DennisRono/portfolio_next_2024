# JEPA / Predictive Latent Modeling

Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA) represents a paradigm shift in how AI systems learn to understand and predict the world around them.

## What is JEPA?

JEPA is an architecture that learns by predicting missing or masked information in an abstract representation space, rather than in pixel space. This approach enables more efficient and robust learning of world models.

## Key Concepts

### Predictive Latent Modeling

Predictive latent modeling focuses on learning representations that capture the underlying structure of data, enabling prediction of future states or missing information in a learned latent space.

### World Models

JEPA contributes to the development of world models—internal representations that AI systems can use to simulate and reason about the environment.

## Applications

- Video understanding and prediction
- Robotics and embodied AI
- Planning and reasoning systems

## Future Directions

The continued development of JEPA and related architectures promises to advance AI systems capable of more human-like reasoning and understanding of the physical world.
