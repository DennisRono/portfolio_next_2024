# Explainable AI

Explainable AI (XAI) encompasses methods and techniques that make AI system outputs understandable to humans, addressing the "black box" nature of many modern AI models.

## What is Explainable AI?

XAI aims to create AI systems whose decisions can be understood, interpreted, and trusted by humans, bridging the gap between powerful AI capabilities and human comprehension.

## Why Explainability Matters

### Trust

Users need to understand AI decisions to trust and appropriately rely on them.

### Accountability

Organizations must be able to explain AI-driven decisions, especially in regulated industries.

### Debugging

Understanding how models work enables identification and correction of errors and biases.

### Compliance

Regulations like GDPR include "right to explanation" requirements for automated decisions.

## XAI Techniques

### Feature Attribution

Methods like SHAP and LIME that identify which input features most influenced a prediction.

### Attention Visualization

Visualizing where models focus attention in inputs like images or text.

### Concept-Based Explanations

Explaining decisions in terms of human-understandable concepts rather than raw features.

### Counterfactual Explanations

Showing what would need to change for a different outcome.

## Approaches

### Inherently Interpretable Models

Using model architectures that are naturally understandable (decision trees, linear models).

### Post-hoc Explanations

Generating explanations for complex models after training.

## Applications

- Healthcare decision support
- Financial credit decisions
- Criminal justice risk assessment
- Autonomous vehicle decisions

## Challenges

- Trade-offs between accuracy and interpretability
- Evaluating explanation quality
- Explanations that are meaningful to different audiences
